      Industry:
      • E-commerce being undeveloped, people still shop in stores (93.1% in 2018)
      • Mexican gas prices are now largely set by the market. Yet the market receives fiscal stimulus/ fuel subsidies, decreasing the ecise tax and providing some govt. control.
      • BP sees the Latin American opportunity as huge
      • Clean energy sources are growing 'massively' in Latin America
      • Price controls often do not protect the poor, causing alternative vouchers and direct cash transfer policies to be implemented as BONOGAS in Dominican Republican; the LPG subsidy reform in El Salvador; or electricity prices in Brazil;
      Market:
      • Healthy living as a new trend for Latin American consumers, how does this adjust the convenience stores merchandise?
      • Growth of Latin American middle class
      • Small retailers account for 40% of retail sales in Latin America, unlike large retail outlets other countries have
      • Mexico plans to focus on oil refining, while continuing to decrease oil and gas production, while also unfavorable fuel price policies
      • Mexico is cracking down on fuel theft, but is causing a shortage of fuel available, long waits for consumers to obtain and the threat of rationing.
      • recently elected Mexico President Manuel Lopez Obrador won on a platform to freeze fuel rpices for 3 years

      Consumer:
      • Trust as a key factor for Latin American consumers and transparency building trust
      • Winning companies in Latin America pay close attention to in-store execution, salesperson accountability (POS execution)/ operations and are more likely to use advanced analytics
      • Winning companies in Latin America are more likely to track SKU-level price gaps on competitors at least on a monthly basis and measure price elasticity in a granularity way
      • Consumers seek to recreate out-of-home experiences such as restaurant style meals or cinema stye snacks
      • Winning companies in Latin America have a broader portfolio of products within the stores, and achieve scale
      • Winning companies in Latin America are more likely to centralize the management of pricing and promotional spending in a single team at the business-unit level
      Competitor:
      • Retail convenience stores have strong relationships with distributors, often determining the products available for sale
